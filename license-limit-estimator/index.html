License Limit Estimator

Estimate whether your working capital and net worth may support a requested Tennessee contractor license monetary limit.

Tennessee contractor limits are generally tied to the lesser of working capital and net worth, with different treatment for renewals.

Amounts due within about a year. Do not list the same debt twice elsewhere.

Everything on the balance sheet. Often larger than current assets because it includes equipment and other long-term assets.

All liabilities on the statement. Split between current and long-term portions when possible.

Enter only the portion of receivables that are more than 90 days past due. This amount is subtracted from current assets. Do not subtract it again from total assets.

Enter the unused line of credit you may rely on in the filing. It is not already counted in current liabilities. This estimator adds it to working capital only, not net worth. If working capital is negative before the LOC, only 50% of the LOC may count.

Optional personal or parent-company support documented for the filing. Enter the gross support amount. This estimator counts 50% toward both working capital and net worth. It does not replace the entity's own numbers on the balance sheet.
