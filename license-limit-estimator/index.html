License Limit Estimator

Estimate whether your working capital and net worth may support a requested Tennessee contractor license monetary limit.

Tennessee contractor limits are generally tied to the lesser of working capital and net worth, with different treatment for renewals.

Read how Tennessee license limits work

Before you start

Gather figures from the balance sheet for the business or entity applying for the license. Use the same entity you plan to list on the application. Do not enter annual revenue.

You will generally need:

  • Current assets
  • Current liabilities
  • Total assets
  • Total liabilities
  • Receivables over 90 days old, if any
  • Unused line of credit or guarantor support, if you plan to rely on them

Optional fields can stay blank if they do not apply. Enter $0 only when the statement truly shows zero.

Choose whether you are applying for a new license, renewing, or asking for a higher limit. Renewals may be reviewed under different rules than initial applications or increases.

Only the part of receivables that are more than 90 days past due. This estimator subtracts that amount from current assets when calculating working capital. It is already inside current assets, so do not subtract it again from total assets.

Unused credit you may document in the filing. It is not already in current liabilities. This estimator adds it to working capital only, not net worth. If working capital is negative before the LOC, only half of the LOC amount may count.

Optional documented support from an owner, parent company, or guarantor. Enter the gross support amount shown in your paperwork. This estimator counts half toward both working capital and net worth. It does not replace the entity's own balance-sheet figures.
